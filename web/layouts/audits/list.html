Target Species (Neglectedness)

Measures focus on underfunded areas, based on the primary beneficiaries of a charity's work. In Hong Kong, some cause areas receive significantly more funding and attention than others.

  • High: Focus on highly neglected areas like Farmed or Wild Animals.
  • Medium: A mix of neglected and saturated areas.
  • Low: Focus on saturated areas like Companion Animals.

Evidence Quality (Tractability)

Indicates the highest level of evidence found for a charity's impact claims, reflecting the problem's tractability.

  • RCT/Meta-Analysis: The highest standard of evidence, often from controlled trials.
  • Quasi-Experimental: Strong evidence, but without full random control.
  • Pre-Post: Evidence based on before-and-after comparisons.
  • Anecdotal/None: Claims are based on stories or lack verifiable evidence.
