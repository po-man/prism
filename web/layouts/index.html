An Audit Engine for Animal Advocacy Analytics

Important Disclaimer: Public Preview [Click to expand/collapse]

Welcome to PRISM. Our goal is to help donors and animal advocates make more informed decisions by providing objective, data-driven analytics for animal charities.

Please note this platform is in a public preview. All content, including financial data and impact metrics, is generated by Artificial Intelligence (AI) by reading public documents and websites. While we aim for high quality, the automated process means the data is not guaranteed to be accurate or complete.

Think of this as a starting point for your own research. Always verify information with the original source documents before making any decisions.

How to Read This Table [Click to expand/collapse]

Target Species (Neglectedness)

This column indicates how much a charity focuses on neglected (i.e., underfunded) animal groups. The opacity of each icon reflects the proportion of beneficiaries from that group. Unevenly distributed funding and attention are very common:

  • Highly Neglected: Farmed and Wild Animals receive comparatively little funding.
  • Saturated: Companion Animals (e.g., dogs and cats) receive the vast majority of funding.
  • Charities focusing on more neglected groups are considered to have higher potential for impact.

Evidence Quality (Tractability)

Indicates the highest level of evidence found for a charity's impact claims, reflecting the problem's tractability.

  • RCT/Meta-Analysis: The highest standard of evidence, often from controlled trials.
  • Quasi-Experimental: Strong evidence, but without full random control.
  • Pre-Post: Evidence based on before-and-after comparisons.
  • Anecdotal/None: Claims are based on stories or lack verifiable evidence.

Audit Summary

Provides a high-level, colour-coded summary (e.g., Pass, Warning, Fail) of an organisation's performance against our automated audit checks. These checks cover areas like financial health, transparency, and governance.

Clicking on an organisation will take you to its detailed profile, which includes a full breakdown of the audit checklist.

Cost per Outcome (USD)

This metric estimates the cost to achieve a single, significant outcome (e.g., one animal's life saved or significantly improved). It's a simplified measure of cost-effectiveness. The icons indicate our confidence in the calculation:

  • {{- partial "icons/checkmark.svg" . | safeHTML -}} High Confidence: Calculated from specific, verifiable program expenses and outcomes.
  • {{- partial "icons/calculator.svg" . | safeHTML -}} Medium Confidence: Estimated by apportioning total organisational expenses to a primary outcome.
  • {{- partial "icons/multi-domains.svg" . | safeHTML -}} Multi-Domain: The organisation works across multiple cause areas, making a single cost-per-outcome calculation impractical.
  • {{- partial "icons/missing-ingredients.svg" . | safeHTML -}} Not Calculated: Insufficient data (e.g., missing financial reports or beneficiary numbers) to perform the calculation.

A lower number is generally better, but this metric should be considered alongside other factors like evidence quality and the scale of operations.
